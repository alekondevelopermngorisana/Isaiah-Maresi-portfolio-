Youth Employability · Entrepreneurship

Youth Employability & Entrepreneurship Training

📍 Nairobi County, Kenya  ·  Oct 2023 – Present  ·  SHOFCO
The Problem

Many recent graduates and youth in Nairobi's marginalised slum communities struggled to translate their skills and ambition into jobs or businesses — held back by weak CVs, limited interview confidence, and little exposure to job market expectations or entrepreneurial fundamentals.

Isaiah's Role

As Sustainable Livelihoods Officer, Isaiah designs and facilitates monthly employability and entrepreneurship trainings, sources appropriate placements for participants, and provides direct mentorship to young entrepreneurs and small business owners.

Key Activities
  • Facilitated monthly job-readiness training covering resume writing, professional communication, interview skills, networking, and workplace etiquette for 5,000+ youth
  • Delivered entrepreneurship training on idea generation, business modelling, market research, financial planning, and pitching
  • Conducted business mentorship to 300+ young entrepreneurs and small business owners in urban slums
  • Built and maintained relationships with 15+ local employers and community organizations to secure job placements and internships
  • Trained 6 refugee groups (100+ members) under the Re-Build program on USLA methodology, entrepreneurship, and financial literacy
Outcomes
5,000+ Youth trained
500+ Job search effectiveness gains
50% Increase in job placement rate
30% Increase in entrepreneurial ventures
20% Attendees who started a business
Partners
SHOFCO Re-Build Program Local Employer Network
02
Financial Inclusion · Community Mobilisation

Shofco Urban Network (SUN) Self-Help Group Facilitation

📍 Mukuru & Nairobi Informal Settlements  ·  2020 – 2023  ·  SHOFCO
The Problem

Urban poor households in Nairobi's informal settlements lacked organized social and financial structures, leaving individuals isolated and without savings mechanisms or a collective voice — especially during the disruption of the COVID-19 period.

Isaiah's Role

As SUN Program Facilitator and later Lead Facilitator, Isaiah brought individuals and households together into Self-Help Groups (SHGs), training leaders and members on group dynamics, leadership, financial literacy, and income-generating activities.

Key Activities
  • Grew SHG membership from 30 to 500+ groups within one year through new mobilization techniques introduced during COVID-19
  • Established a Ksh.50 savings challenge campaign across 90+ SHGs, increasing saving culture and group attendance by 80%
  • Delivered training modules to 750+ SHG members on financial management, entrepreneurship, and gender empowerment
  • Assisted in the registration of 50+ self-help groups with the SDO Department for legal recognition
  • Marketed SHOFCO SACCO products and recruited 500+ members into the SACCO within two years
Outcomes
90+ SHGs facilitated
750+ Members trained
80% Increase in saving culture
500+ SACCO members recruited
40+ SHGs became sustainable enterprises
Partners
SHOFCO SHOFCO SACCO Department of Social Development

Leadership Development · Coaching

Weekly Leadership Coaching for Young Leaders

📍 Nairobi County, Kenya  ·  Apr 2025 – Present  ·  Taifa Teule Network
The Problem

Many young people in community groups, youth programs, and organizations had the potential to lead but lacked the mindset, structured practice, and confidence to respond to challenges and take initiative rather than remain passive observers.

Isaiah's Role

As Volunteer Senior Leadership Facilitator, Isaiah designs and facilitates interactive leadership workshops every Saturday, focused on critical thinking, accountability, problem-solving, and practical leadership tools.

Key Activities
  • Designed and facilitated structured, high-impact leadership sessions for emerging leaders
  • Introduced practical leadership tools that improved confidence, communication, and decision-making
  • Mentored young leaders to initiate and sustain community-based projects aligned with the program's vision
  • Fostered a collaborative environment encouraging peer-to-peer leadership growth
Outcomes
200+ Leaders trained overall
50+ Leaders in structured sessions
High Participant satisfaction rating
Partners
Taifa Teule Network

Global Mentorship · Career Readiness

Global Volunteer Mentorship for Career Readiness

📍 Remote  ·  Jan 2026 – Present  ·  Global Mentorship Initiative
The Problem

College students and early-career professionals around the world — especially those in the non-profit and social impact sector — often lack structured guidance and confidence-building support as they prepare for their first career roles.

Isaiah's Role

As a Global Volunteer Mentor, Isaiah provides career guidance and structured virtual mentorship to mentees worldwide, helping them build confidence, set career goals, and navigate opportunities.

Key Activities
  • Resume building and interview preparation guidance
  • AI-powered job search strategies and professional networking on LinkedIn
  • Career planning using SMART goals and global business communication skills
  • Coaching on how to excel in the workplace environment and job search best practices
